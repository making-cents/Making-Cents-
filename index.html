Why do footballers earn more than nurses?
The market does not pay you according to social utility. Wage determination follows the logic of scarcity, a principle with implications far beyond professional sport.

Why has the cost of living risen so sharply?
Price inflation across essential goods — food, energy, housing — has affected household budgets at every income level. Here is what drove it and what the data shows.

Why is homeownership increasingly inaccessible?
Property ownership was once achievable on a single median wage. An examination of house price growth, stagnating real wages, and supply constraints explains the structural shift.

About this platform

Making Cents was built on a simple frustration: economics shapes so much in the world around us — wages, rent, opportunity, political decisions — and yet it is rarely explained to the people most affected by it.

This platform exists to change that. Every concept is written from first principles, assumes no prior knowledge, and is honest about where genuine uncertainty or disagreement exists among economists. The aim is clarity, and a first insight for those who are curious about understanding the science that shapes the world around them.

FAQs
Do I need any prior knowledge of economics to use this? +
No. Every concept is written from first principles. If a term requires understanding a previous concept, that concept is explained first. You can begin anywhere in the course and follow the links where needed.
Who is this course designed for? +
Making Cents is written primarily for readers aged 13 to 15, though the content is accessible to anyone who wants a plain-English introduction to economics. The concepts are the same ones discussed in news coverage and public debate — explained without assumption of prior knowledge.
How long does each lesson take? +
Most lessons are designed to be read in three to five minutes. The goal is to explain one concept clearly and completely — not to be comprehensive at the expense of clarity.
Can I suggest a concept to be covered? +
Yes. If there is an economic term or idea you have encountered and would like explained, use the Contact page to submit a suggestion. Reader suggestions directly inform what gets covered next.

Who we are

Economics is present in almost every significant decision a society makes — about wages, housing, healthcare, education, and opportunity. Yet for most young people, the subject remains inaccessible: either too academic in its framing, or too simplified to be genuinely useful.

Making Cents was founded on a straightforward premise: that a 13-year-old who hears the word "recession" on the news deserves a clear, honest account of what it means — not a dismissal, and not a textbook definition stripped of context.

The goal is not to produce economists. It is to produce citizens who understand the forces shaping their lives.

¢

What we stand for

I

Plain language, always.

Technical vocabulary is used only where it is the most precise available tool — and always explained on first use. Complexity is never a substitute for clarity.

II

Grounded in the familiar.

Every concept is introduced through something the reader already has experience of — a price, a pay slip, a headline. Abstraction follows, not precedes, understanding.

III

Honest about uncertainty.

Economics contains genuine disputes. Where the evidence is contested or incomplete, this platform says so — and explains why reasonable people disagree.
